Variation 1
Straight Rows
Each seller sits in their own row with their car. The buyer gets one column on the right: four gray versions of those same cars, all looking identical. Cleanest to read at a glance.

What works: Row-by-row alignment makes it crystal clear which seller owns which car. The gray cars in the right column mirror the colored cars one-for-one — same shape, same position, just stripped of information. The buyer's summary sits at the bottom right, naturally where the eye lands last.

One decision: The buyer column currently shows 4 separate gray cars. Alternative: collapse them into one car image with a "×4" label, emphasizing that to the buyer they're indistinguishable.

What works: The big bubble on the right creates a strong visual contrast with the left side — colorful and differentiated vs. gray and uniform. The cloud dots (thought bubble convention) make it feel more casual and teen-friendly. The 2×2 grid inside the bubble emphasizes "these all look the same" more than a column would.

One decision: The dividing line is subtle here. Could add a lock icon on it, or label it "Information barrier."
Variation 3
Connected by Arrows
Same seller-car-buyer layout, but light arrows run from each seller to their car, and from the cars to the buyer. The arrows going to the buyer all converge on a single question-mark bubble — visually showing that information flows freely from seller to car, but gets blocked before it reaches the buyer.

What works: The red ✕ marks on the barrier make it visceral and clear that information is being actively blocked, not just absent. The dashed vertical line is a classic "wall" convention. The cloud dots on the left side of the buyer bubble indicate the thought is coming from the buyer, not from the cars.

One decision: The ✕ marks next to each blocked arrow could instead be 🔇 or 🚫 icons for more personality. Also, the arrows from sellers → cars could have the tier color (gold, green, orange, red) to reinforce that the cars carry known information — it just doesn't pass the wall.
